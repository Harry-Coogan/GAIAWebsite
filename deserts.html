Deserts

Deserts cover about 20 to 30 percent of Earthâ€™s land surface and are found on every continent.

Deserts receive very little rain each year, usually less than 10 inches (25 centimeters) of rainfall.

Deserts have different types of soil, such as sand, gravel, or rock, and different land features, such as dunes, mountains, or plains.

Deserts have extremes of temperature and very little plant growth.

Deserts are home to around 1 billion people and many organisms that are adapted to the dry climate.
